OUR VISION

In one of the sessions in 1970 in University of Ibadan, the Lord showed Pastor Olu AriJesudade, a vision that a torrent was sweeping away some youths. He shared the vision with his mates after the prayers. There was an encouragement from his prayer group members to discuss the vision with the founder of CAC Bethel, late Prophetess J.O Ogunranti who prayed and enlightened them on the mind of God. Her interpretation being that the youths of the Church were being swept away by the torrent of worldliness and that God was looking for someone who would stand in the gap like Noah who was raised to build and to preserve the righteous in that generation from the flood. So, Pastor Samuel AriJesudade was raised as an Apostle for the youths of Christ Apostolic Church.

On confirmation and knowing the magnitude of the vision, late prophetess Ogunranti a staff nurse and the leader of Christ Apostolic Church, Bethel took the report to the President of the CAC mission in person of late Pastor E.T. Latunde who sought God's confirmation by contacting other notable prophets of the Church, such as Late Pastor Akinade and Obadare.

The confirmation of the vision made the Supreme Council of the Church to approve the name of the group-Christ Apostolic Church Students' Association as an evangelical youth organ of the mission. The group was registered with the authorities of University of Ibadan in 1971. Professor A.M.A Imevbore became the Patron while late Prophetess J.O Ogunranti became the patroness of CACSA. The early members were very studious and embarked on aggressive Evangelism. The association took root in other higher institutions like OAU, lle-Ife and Mission secondary Schools in the south western part of the country. Members would hold meetings and programmes during holidays. Young men and women who were uneducated joined the local assembliesâ€™ branches in large number to study the word and pray. In furtherance of the gospel, members who were to serve in the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) offered to serve in the mission owned schools sacrificially as field/travelling evangelists.
